This page shows a series of other projects I have undertaken in a diverse range of media.



Water pump made for ANU's Manufacturing Technologies course to explore a range of manufacturing techniques and design decisions. I learned to use new tools and processes including drill pressing, bandsawing, and sand casting.


The Build a Skull Challenge is an interactive website I created to help undergrad students learn and revise anatomical concepts, complementing ANU's Human Anatomy course. I programmed the interactive content in Unity and embedded it in a website. This site has since been used as a teaching resource in the course BIOL3186.


One example of a practical project was that I built a ramp to replace a single step that was in a doorway. The step location made it very difficult to notice, and it was therefore a tripping hazard. This also made the house more accessible.


As well as creating practical items from wood, I like to make asthetic pieces. This kangaroo is build with hinged legs and balanced such that when placed on a slope, it 'hops' along.


With a competitive sister who loves cats, this cat stacking challenge was an entertaining gift! This was also a great opportunity to try out a Carvey CNC router at the MakerSpace.


I found that canvas shoes are an excellent medium for embroidery. I gifted these as a subtle tip to an anime, and they were well worn.


Examples of sewing cartoon characters. When I create such characters I work from reference images rather than an existing pattern.


Mosaic bee bath to provide bees a place to drink, with rocks so they won't drown.


Metal butterfly garden ornaments. The borders of the metal were coated in solder to remove sharp edges.


A different take on the Dungeons and Dragons staple dice set, to hone my 3D printing skills.
